Processing Art

"Colored Trail"

(move the mouse around and click to change colors)

. . .

I wanted to create a rainbow trail that also created some type of particle effect that would

disappear. However, the particle effect seemed too complicated to convert it into a P5 code for

me, so I just stuck with the rainbow trail. I decided to create an interactive art like this because

I wanted my artwork to appear as playful and reminiscent of something childish for the users.

. . .

The embedded HTML below is what I had in my original Processing code. I decided to upload it

onto OpenProcessing.org since I was having difficulties trying to screen capture it on my laptop.

. . .

Going further in depth with Processing, it was easy to follow the lessons until math got

involved. I ended up using other artists' codes to help create my own interactive P5 art,

To make it my own, I changed the coloring, some of the code of the trail to keep its

saturated colors, and some of the code of the bubbles to not have outlines as well as

sort of let the circles pulse when it is dragged. I also changed the sizing of the circles.
